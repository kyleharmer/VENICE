⚠️ ST. MARK'S BASILICA DRESS CODE: Shoulders & knees must be covered. Security enforces strictly—no shorts, sleeveless shirts, or short skirts. Carry a light scarf as backup.
Main Activity
Doge's Palace & St. Mark's Basilica
~2 hours | Venice's political & religious heart
What you'll see: Doge's Palace: Gilded state rooms, enormous Tintoretto paintings, the "staircase of the giants," Bridge of Sighs. St. Mark's: Byzantine mosaics, Pala d'Oro, holy reliquaries, soaring domes.
Why it matters: This was Venice's political and spiritual center for over 1,000 years. Where decisions that shaped Europe were made. St. Mark's is one of the world's most famous churches.
Practical: Doge's: 3 floors, elevators available. Basilica: ground level, crowded. Buy tickets online. 1 hour Doge's + 0.5-1 hour Basilica. Busy 11am-4pm; go early or late.
Line 1 westbound from Arsenale Get off San Marco-Vallaresso or San Marco-Giardinetti (5-6 stops, ~15 min). See clock tower and Basilica dome. Exit toward the square.

Morning on Burano (11:15 AM–1:00 PM)
Colorful Houses, Canals & First Exploration
~1.5 hours | Photography, wander, breathe it in
What you'll see: Rows of vibrantly painted fisherman's houses lining the canals. Piazza Baldassarre Galuppi (the main square with church, town hall, shops). Quiet alleys away from the main drag. Watch lace artisans at work in small shops—no pressure to buy, just watch the craft. Small artisan boutiques (maps, local glass, masks).
Why it matters: Burano is THE island for Instagrammable color and authentic island life. Less touristy than Venice, more relaxed vibe. The houses were painted bright so fishermen could spot them from the water—pure practicality turned into beauty.
Practical: Wheelchair-accessible main paths and Piazza. Narrow alleys are tight but manageable. Bring sunscreen and a hat. 1.5 hours gives you time to settle in, photograph, and watch lace demonstrations before lunch.

Afternoon (2:30–4:30 PM)
Lace Shops, Pescarìa Vecia, Golden Hour
~2 hours | Deeper exploration, artisan crafts, sunset prep
What to do: Browse lace shops on quieter streets (not the main tourist strip near vaporetto). Watch artisans at work—the detail is breathtaking. Visit Pescarìa Vecia (old fish market) for sunset photo opportunities. Wander canals you missed. Sit by the water with a spritz. Let the island rhythm slow you down.
Why lace matters: Burano lace is a 500+ year tradition. Real, handmade lace is expensive—that's normal. The pieces in quieter shops tend to be more authentic than tourist-focused items. Supporting local artisans means something here.
Photo tip: Late afternoon light (3–5pm) is golden. Best photos of the colorful houses + reflections in water happen now. Bring your camera or phone fully charged.

Main Activity
Peggy Guggenheim Museum
~2 hours | Modern art in a palazzo garden
What you'll see: Peggy's personal collection: Pollock, Kandinsky, Dali, Ernst, Magritte. Her unfinished palazzo, sculpture garden. Intimate rooms where she lived with her art.
Why it matters: Peggy was one of the 20th century's greatest art patrons. She saved modern art from the Nazis, discovered Jackson Pollock, and moved to Venice. Deeply personal.
Practical: Ground floor + outdoor garden. No major staircases, very accessible. Smallest and least crowded museum. Cafe in garden (pricey but nice). 1.5-2 hours good.
Line 1 westbound from Arsenale Get off Accademia (6-8 stops, ~20 min). Walk south (~10 min). Left bank of Grand Canal in Dorsoduro. Look for Palazzo Venier dei Leoni sign.

Main Activity
Gallerie dell'Accademia
~2 hours | Venice's Renaissance collection
What you'll see: 24 rooms of chronologically-arranged Venetian art from 14th-18th centuries. Titian, Bellini, Veronese, Tintoretto. The Leonardo Vitruvian Man. Masterpieces.
Why it matters: The definitive museum of Venetian art. Watch the evolution of Venetian painting across 400 years, room by room.
Practical: Multiple staircases, 24 rooms. Can feel overwhelming—lots of art. Busiest museum. 2 hours realistic. Pick 5-6 paintings, ignore the rest. Buy tickets online.
Line 1 westbound from Arsenale Get off Accademia (6-8 stops, ~20 min). See the wooden bridge. Museum entrance right there on waterfront.
