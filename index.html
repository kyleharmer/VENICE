Main Activity
Doge's Palace & St. Mark's Basilica
~2 hours | Venice's political & religious heart
What you'll see: Doge's Palace: Gilded state rooms, enormous Tintoretto paintings, the "staircase of the giants," secret corridors, and the Bridge of Sighs. St. Mark's Basilica: Byzantine mosaics, the Pala d'Oro (jeweled gold altarpiece), holy reliquaries, soaring domes.
Why it matters: This was Venice's political and spiritual center for over 1,000 years. The Doge's Palace is where decisions that shaped Europe were made. St. Mark's is one of the world's most famous churches.
Physical & practical: Doge's Palace: 3 floors with staircases, elevators available. Basilica: ground level, often crowded. Buy tickets online. 1 hour Doge's + 0.5-1 hour Basilica. Very busy 11am-4pm; go early or late.
VAPORETTO: Line 1 westbound from Arsenale Get off at San Marco-Vallaresso or San Marco-Giardinetti (5-6 stops, about 15 min). You'll see the clock tower (Campanile) and the Basilica dome. Exit toward the square.

Main Activity
Jewish Ghetto & Cannaregio Wandering
~2.5 hours | First European ghetto + local neighborhoods
What you'll see: Campo del Ghetto Nuovo where Venice's Jewish community lived starting in 1516. Five historic synagogues built vertically. Museum exhibits, kosher restaurants. Quiet canals, local bars, real Venetian life.
Why it matters: First Jewish ghetto in Europe. Powerful history. The community is still active here. Walking these narrow streets feels like stepping back in time. Cannaregio is where regular Venetians live.
Physical & practical: Small ghetto square. Alleys are narrow (wheelchair-passable but snug). Synagogue interiors have stairs (skip if needed). Magic is wandering. 1 hour ghetto + 1-1.5 hours wandering. Very quiet.
VAPORETTO: Line 1 westbound from Arsenale Get off at San Marcuola (12-13 stops, about 25 min). Walk north into Cannaregio. Follow signs to "Ghetto." About 10-15 min walk, or walk directly from apartment through Cannaregio.

Main Activity
Peggy Guggenheim Museum
~2 hours | Modern art collection in a palazzo garden
What you'll see: Peggy's personal collection: Jackson Pollock, Kandinsky, Dali, Ernst, Magritte. Her unfinished palazzo, sculpture garden. Intimate rooms where she lived with her art.
Why it matters: Peggy was one of the 20th century's greatest art patrons. She saved modern art from the Nazis, discovered Jackson Pollock, and moved to Venice. Deeply personal—her taste and legacy.
Physical & practical: Ground floor galleries + outdoor garden. No major staircases, very accessible. Smallest and least crowded museum. Cafe in garden is pricey but nice place to sit. 1.5-2 hours good.
VAPORETTO: Line 1 westbound from Arsenale Get off at Accademia (6-8 stops, about 20 min). Walk south from dock (about 10 min). On left bank of Grand Canal in Dorsoduro. Look for Palazzo Venier dei Leoni sign.

Main Activity
Scuola Grande di San Rocco
~1.5-2 hours | 56 Tintoretto paintings
What you'll see: 56 monumental Tintoretto paintings covering walls and ceilings across two floors. Ornate wooden ceilings, marble staircases, gilded details. Tintoretto spent 23 years here.
Why it matters: Tintoretto's masterpiece. If you love Renaissance art, pilgrimage site. Scale and ambition overwhelming. Paintings cover every surface—you spend time looking up.
Physical & practical: Two levels with marble staircases. No elevator, but steps are wide and well-maintained. Can feel crowded. Go early or late. Don't try to see everything—pick a few paintings to focus on.
VAPORETTO: Line 1 westbound from Arsenale Get off at San Toma (7 stops, about 20 min). Walk into San Polo (northwest). Scuola nearby (about 5 min walk). Next to Frari church.

Main Activity
Gallerie dell'Accademia
~2 hours | Venice's Renaissance art collection
What you'll see: 24 rooms of chronologically-arranged Venetian art from 14th-18th centuries. Titian, Bellini, Veronese, Tintoretto. The Leonardo Vitruvian Man. Masterpieces.
Why it matters: The definitive museum of Venetian art. You can watch the evolution of Venetian painting across 400 years, room by room.
Physical & practical: Multiple staircases, 24 rooms. Can feel overwhelming—lots of art. Busiest museum of your trip. 2 hours realistic. Pick 5-6 paintings and ignore the rest. Buy tickets online.
